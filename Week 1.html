The Random Walk                                                                                       

As with other problems in physics, e.g. to determine the path of gas particles, the random walk model can be used to calculate an average value of Re, using statistical arguments.

The problem of random walks is clearly presented below

A man starts from a point O and walks l yard in a straight line; he then turns through any angle whatever and walks another l yards in a second straight line. He repeats this process n times. I require the probability that after these n stretches he is at a distance between r and r+dr from this starting point O

Pearson, Nature 1905

It is evident why the random walk is also known as "drunkard's walk".

It has been already mentioned that the freely jointed model is unrealistic since it does not take into account for bond angles nor for the existence of preferred conformations. However, for real chains, restricted rotations cannot be neglected e.g. tendency to adopt low energy trans and gauche states. All these effects can be taken into account as indicated by the example below.
